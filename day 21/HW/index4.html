Life of Pi

Life of Pi (2012), directed by Ang Lee and based on Yann Martel's bestselling novel, is a visually stunning and thought-provoking film that blends adventure, spirituality, and survival into a breathtaking cinematic experience. The story follows the journey of Pi Patel, a young Indian boy who survives a shipwreck and finds himself stranded in the Pacific Ocean on a lifeboat with an unlikely companion—a Bengal tiger named Richard Parker.

Pi Patel (played by Suraj Sharma) is the son of a zoo owner in Pondicherry, India. His life is defined by his love for animals and his spiritual curiosity, as he practices Hinduism, Christianity, and Islam simultaneously, much to the confusion of his family and community. When Pi’s family decides to emigrate to Canada, they board a ship, bringing some of their zoo animals along. Tragically, the ship encounters a fierce storm and sinks, leaving Pi as the sole human survivor.
